About the world of Coffee

What is Coffee? Coffee is a brewed beverage made from the seeds of the Coffea plant, commonly referred to as coffee beans. These seeds are roasted and then ground to produce the aromatic drink enjoyed by millions around the world. Coffee contains caffeine, a stimulant that provides a temporary energy boost and enhances alertness.

Types of Coffee Beans.

There are two main species of coffee beans:

Arabica (Coffea arabica): Known for its smooth, slightly acidic flavor and a wider range of aromatic profiles, Arabica beans are typically regarded as higher quality.

Robusta (Coffea canephora): Has a stronger, bolder flavor with more bitterness and is higher in caffeine content. It's commonly used in espresso blends.

Coffee in Europe

1600s: Coffee reached Europe through trade routes. Venice was one of the first European cities to establish coffeehouses, which became centers for intellectual and social exchange.

Coffeehouses in England: By the 17th century, London had many coffeehouses, and these spaces were crucial to the development of modern British society. Coffeehouses were also called “Penny Universities” due to the intellectual discussions that occurred there. Global Expansion and Colonialism

Dutch Colonization (1600s): The Dutch first successfully cultivated coffee outside of Arabia by transplanting coffee plants to Java (modern-day Indonesia).

French & Spanish Plantations: During the 18th century, European colonists introduced coffee plants to their tropical colonies in the Americas and the Caribbean.

Brazilian Dominance (19th Century): By the 1800s, Brazil became the world’s leading producer of coffee, a position it still holds today.

Coffee in the Modern World

Industrial Revolution (19th Century): Coffee became more widely accessible due to improved production methods and mass transportation, including the invention of the coffee filter by Melitta Bentz in 1908.

20th Century: Coffee consumption surged worldwide, with innovations like instant coffee in the 1930s, espresso machines, and the rise of chain coffeehouses like Starbucks in the late 20th century.
