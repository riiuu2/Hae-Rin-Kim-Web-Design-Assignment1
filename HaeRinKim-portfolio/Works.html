History of Coffee

Origins and Early Use

Ethiopia (9th Century): The first known discovery of coffee is linked to the legend of Kaldi, a goat herder who noticed his goats became energetic after eating coffee cherries. It was in the region of Ethiopia where coffee is believed to have originated.

Early Coffee Use: Coffee likely spread from Ethiopia into the Arabian Peninsula in the 15th century, where it was first used as a beverage.

Coffee in the Arab World

15th-16th Century: Coffee became a popular beverage in Yemen, where it was cultivated and traded. The Arabian Peninsula is where coffee first entered the broader world, and it became integral to Islamic culture, especially in religious and social gatherings.

Ottoman Empire: The first coffeehouse, known as a qahveh khaneh, was established in Istanbul around 1555, and coffee quickly spread through the Ottoman Empire and into Europe.
